What is GENI

GENI is a web-based portal for managing Seattle resources. These resources are donated by users who have installed Seattle on their machines. In return these users are allowed to use resources donated by others. GENI enables users of donated resources to acquire nodes around the world on which they can then execute programs written in Repy, the Seattle platform programming language. GENI also lets users to share their resources with other users, and perform a variety of other tasks. For more information about about how GENI fits with the rest of the Seattle platform, see this page.

How to Register

You are required to fill out five fields as part of the registration process.

  • Username. The login name you want to use in the GENI portal. You will need your username as well as your password to login to the portal.
  • Password. You should choose a password that is at least 6 characters in length. Please secure your password by making it obscure enough to evade possible dictionary attacks.
  • Password Confirmation. Confirm your password.
  • Affiliation. Choose a company name or an institution that you associate with.
  • My Public Key. If you have a personal public/private key pair that you would like to use to access Seattle nodes, please select "Let me upload my public key" and select to upload your public key by clicking "Browse.." in the field below. Alternatively, you can have GENI generate the public/private key pair for you.

How to Login

Enter your username and password to log into the portal.
